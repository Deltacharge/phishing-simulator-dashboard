🧠 How to Protect Yourself

1

Verify the sender's email address

Always check the actual email address, not just the display name. Attackers often use similar-looking domains.

2

Don't click links in urgent emails

Phishing emails create urgency ("your password will expire in 24 hours"). Navigate to the site directly instead of clicking email links.

3

Check the URL before entering credentials

Always verify you're on the correct domain before typing your username or password. Look for HTTPS and the correct company domain.

4

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Even if your password is compromised, MFA adds an extra layer of protection that can prevent unauthorized access.

5

Report suspicious emails

When in doubt, report the email to your IT security team. It's always better to be cautious than compromised.

🚨 Red Flags in the Email You Received

  • Urgency and fear tactics ("password will expire in 24 hours")
  • Generic greeting or mismatched sender identity
  • Link URL doesn't match the official company domain
  • Request to enter credentials on an external page
  • Threat of account lockout to pressure immediate action
